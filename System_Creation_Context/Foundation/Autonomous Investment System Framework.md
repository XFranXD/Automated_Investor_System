# **Architecting the Targeted Baseline Engine (TBE): A Capital Preservation Framework for Level 4 Autonomous Investment**

The design of a Level 4 autonomous investment system necessitates a fundamental paradigm shift away from traditional algorithmic trading models. While high-frequency and standard quantitative systems frequently optimize for signal-to-trade execution speed and absolute profit maximization, the Targeted Baseline Engine (TBE) framework is architected strictly around automated investment judgment. The mandate dictates a paramount focus on capital preservation, portfolio longevity, and stringent risk control. The system must optimize the overall portfolio rather than individual trades, heavily favor lower drawdowns over absolute returns, strictly limit holding periods to a 2–20 day swing horizon, and operate under the premise that remaining out of the market—abstention—is a successful defensive posture rather than a failure state.  
To achieve this, the TBE architecture enforces an immutable boundary between qualitative reasoning and deterministic execution. Artificial Intelligence (AI) serves exclusively as an interpretive engine, designed for synthesizing narratives, extracting semantic meaning from unstructured events, and formulating hypotheses based on confirmed data. Conversely, all mathematics, statistical analysis, risk modeling, and trade execution mechanics are locked within compiled, deterministic code1. Learning remains strictly observational, utilizing closed-trade data to refine parameters cumulatively without ever sacrificing live capital for reinforcement exploration.  
The following report exhaustively details the architectural blueprint for TBE. It evaluates analytical methodologies tailored to the 2–20 day horizon, establishes a rigorous market regime detection mechanism, formalizes the two-tier evidence and disqualification hierarchy, delineates the AI-code boundary, constructs a capital-preservation-first risk architecture, and defines the immutable architectural laws derived from the most severe institutional failures in modern financial history.

## **Market Regime Recognition Architecture**

In an autonomous system prioritizing capital preservation, standard assumptions regarding asset correlation, volatility, and liquidity can degrade rapidly. Institutional risk frameworks dictate that a system must dynamically identify the prevailing market regime to ensure its operational baseline remains valid2. A strategy that yields steady compounding in a low-volatility expansion phase will suffer catastrophic drawdowns in a highly correlated panic. Financial markets exhibit non-stationary behavior, meaning that predictive models and risk parameters must be recalibrated when the underlying structural regime shifts4.  
Professional investors and quantitative analysts typically divide market conditions into broad archetypes: steady trend, noisy range (mean-reverting), calm drift, and high-volatility/crisis2. TBE requires concrete, deterministic metrics to classify these regimes without relying on AI inference. The optimal approach utilizes a composite regime-detection matrix based on advanced statistical indicators.  
The first critical metric is the Turbulence Index. Traditional volatility metrics like the VIX measure the magnitude of price movements but fail to capture structural breakdowns in asset relationships. The Turbulence Index measures the statistical unusualness of cross-sectional asset returns by computing the Mahalanobis distance of current returns against a historical covariance matrix7. A spike in the Turbulence Index indicates that historically uncorrelated assets are converging or that historically correlated assets are diverging, which is a classic hallmark of a liquidity crisis, panic, or systemic transition7.  
The second critical metric is the Absorption Ratio. Developed as a measure of systemic market fragility, the Absorption Ratio calculates the fraction of total variance within a set of asset returns that is explained or "absorbed" by a finite number of principal components or eigenvectors11. When markets become tightly coupled, the Absorption Ratio spikes, indicating that the market is highly fragile and susceptible to systemic shocks. TBE's deterministic code should actively monitor the 15-day moving average of the Absorption Ratio against its 1-year average; standardized shifts greater than 1.0 signal imminent drawdowns and dictate an immediate reduction in portfolio exposure12.  
Furthermore, institutional models like State Street's Market Regime Indicator (MRI) use a blend of implied volatility, widening credit spreads, and price momentum to categorize the market into risk-seeking or risk-averse phases6. TBE should incorporate credit spreads—specifically the spread between high-yield corporate debt and safe-haven government bonds—as a deterministic input. Widening spreads inherently signal a transition from expansion to cautious decline or market turmoil6. At the asset-specific level, the Average True Range (ATR) serves as a primary metric for volatility and participation17. Rapidly expanding ATR values across a broad basket of assets confirm a transition into a high-volatility regime, triggering mandatory position sizing reductions.  
When the deterministic regime engine detects a shift from a steady state to a highly fragile state (e.g., a standardized spike in the Absorption Ratio combined with a Turbulence Index reading above the 75th percentile), TBE must automatically execute defensive protocols9. The system's operating assumptions are updated to expect higher cross-asset correlations, resulting in tighter portfolio concentration limits, smaller ATR-adjusted position sizes, and a heightened propensity to abstain from trading entirely.

## **Methodology Inventory for the 2–20 Day Swing Horizon**

Swing trading within a 2–20 day window requires analytical methodologies that bridge the gap between intraday microstructure noise and long-term fundamental valuation19. Not all analytical approaches yield empirical support for this specific horizon, and many introduce unnecessary complexity without improving decision quality. TBE must evaluate all major methodologies based on their ability to generate reliable signals that align strictly with capital preservation.  
Event-Driven and Fundamental methodologies, specifically those focused on Post-Earnings Announcement Drift (PEAD), present the strongest empirical foundation for the 2–20 day horizon. PEAD is one of the most robust, empirically supported anomalies in modern finance, demonstrating that equities systematically underreact to fundamental earnings surprises and subsequently drift in the direction of the surprise for 2 to 75 days22. The anomaly is driven by cognitive dissonance and the delayed repositioning of institutional capital. Research indicates that stocks predominantly followed by long-horizon investors exhibit stronger immediate reactions and a larger, more persistent post-announcement drift23. This behavioral underreaction provides a highly reliable, deterministic trigger that perfectly aligns with TBE's target holding period.  
Technical Analysis, while often dismissed as predictive charting, is absolutely critical when used reactively for execution timing and risk management. Moving averages (e.g., 10-day and 20-day Simple Moving Average crossovers) and volatility channels like the Average True Range (ATR) provide the precise entry and exit boundaries required for swing trading21. While technical analysis does not supply the fundamental "why" of a trade, it complements event-driven strategies by defining the mathematical parameters of the risk-reward ratio and ensuring trades are only entered when short-term momentum aligns with the fundamental catalyst.  
Conversely, pure short-term momentum strategies—chasing 1–3 day price breakouts without fundamental catalysts—frequently suffer from mean-reversion and false breakouts, making them highly vulnerable to whipsaws28. Relying solely on price momentum without fundamental or event-driven confirmation introduces excessive drawdown risk and violates the capital preservation mandate. Similarly, mean-reversion strategies, which involve buying past losers, show some efficacy over a 3–12 month horizon, but applying mean-reversion strictly to a 2–20 day window is mathematically akin to catching falling knives28. Mean-reversion should be excluded as a primary strategy and used only as a secondary filter, such as avoiding over-extended Relative Strength Index (RSI) conditions on entry19.  
Macroeconomic forecasting relies on indicators like GDP growth, employment figures, and CPI data, which unfold over months and quarters31. Attempting to trade a short-term swing based on a long-term macro forecast introduces a severe duration mismatch. Macro data should only be used contextually within the global regime-detection module, not as a direct trade trigger. Sentiment analysis, utilizing natural language processing to extract mood from news and financial reports, provides immediate qualitative context15. It is highly complementary to event-driven strategies, allowing the AI to gauge the semantic tone of a catalyst, though it remains too noisy to act as a standalone trigger.

TBE should construct its investment hypotheses primarily through Event-Driven catalysts combined with Qualitative Sentiment parsing by the AI, while relying on Technical Analysis strictly for deterministic risk sizing and execution.

## **The Evidence Hierarchy: Hard Disqualifiers vs. Scored Evidence**

TBE operates on a locked two-tier evidence model: a set of hard, binary disqualifiers that act as absolute gates, followed by an equal-weighted scoring of surviving evidence. The hard-disqualifier list is the most critical component for capital preservation. It systematically eliminates assets exposing the portfolio to asymmetric, unquantifiable, or systemic tail risks before any scoring or AI reasoning occurs, ensuring that the system is heavily biased toward rejecting opportunities34.  
Drawing from institutional investment policy statements (IPS), fiduciary standards, and corporate governance frameworks, certain conditions represent confirmed severe risks that must trigger an immediate, deterministic rejection of an investment candidate37.  
The Beneish M-Score is an established mathematical model designed to detect earnings manipulation using eight financial ratios, including days sales in receivables, gross margin index, and asset quality index41. A score greater than \-1.78 (or \-2.22 in more conservative applications) indicates a high statistical probability of financial fraud and accounting manipulation. The model famously flagged Enron prior to its collapse while Wall Street analysts were still recommending the stock44. Any asset breaching the \-1.78 threshold must be deterministically gated by TBE, as financial manipulation invalidates all other fundamental data43.  
Similarly, the Altman Z-Score predicts the probability of corporate bankruptcy within a two-year window by synthesizing liquidity, profitability, and solvency ratios47. An asset falling into the "distress zone" (a score strictly below 1.81) presents an insolvency risk that completely violates the capital preservation mandate and must be rejected outright46.  
Corporate governance and regulatory events provide further binary gates. The resignation of an independent auditor, or the issuance of an adverse or disclaimed audit opinion, indicates a fundamental breakdown in financial reporting integrity and must result in immediate disqualification49. The failure to file mandatory financial disclosures (e.g., delayed 10-K or 10-Q filings) obscures critical financial health data and frequently precedes major downward restatements or delisting proceedings, warranting an absolute gate51. Furthermore, the issuance of a formal notice of impending enforcement action by a major regulatory body, such as an SEC Wells Notice, introduces unquantifiable litigation and financial risk52.  
Finally, extreme liquidity constraints must be enforced to prevent the system from becoming trapped in illiquid positions. Candidates must meet minimum Average Daily Volume (ADV) thresholds (e.g., ADV \> 500,000 shares) and maintain a bid-ask spread below a fixed percentage of the asset price19. Any asset triggering the OFAC 50 Percent Rule, indicating direct or indirect ownership by sanctioned entities, presents severe geopolitical and portfolio impairment risks and is instantly gated55.

Conditions that rely on qualitative interpretation, unverified allegations, or pending outcomes belong in the equal-weighted scoring tier rather than the hard-disqualifier list. If an asset passes the hard gates, the AI evaluates these factors, and the deterministic code applies negative weights to the final score without triggering a binary rejection.

## **The AI and Deterministic Code Boundary: Stress-Testing the Divide**

TBE’s architecture mandates a strict separation of concerns: AI is utilized solely for qualitative, interpretive reasoning, while deterministic code manages mathematics, statistics, rules, and execution1. Blurring this boundary introduces catastrophic failure modes, particularly in high-autonomy financial systems.  
When AI is misused for tasks that should be deterministic, the system becomes vulnerable to hallucination and arithmetic failure. Large Language Models (LLMs) struggle with complex arithmetic over large data tables, such as evaluating a 10-K balance sheet to derive an Altman Z-Score or calculating the Average True Range1. Relying on AI for these metrics results in probabilistic math. If an LLM is tasked with position sizing, minor tokenization errors can result in the AI requesting an allocation of 40% of the portfolio rather than 4.0%. To mitigate this, deterministic Python or C++ modules must ingest raw financial APIs, calculate all scores and metrics, and pass boolean results to the system state1. The AI outputs only a qualitative conviction label, which the deterministic code intercepts to mathematically calculate the exact capital allocation using hard-coded formulas.  
In multi-agent autonomous systems, relying purely on AI inference to generate hypotheses creates a feedback loop of instability known as the "agent bullwhip" effect56. When autonomous agents coordinate through delayed and partial information without a structural floor, local narrative hallucinations amplify across the system, leading to run-to-run decision instability and severe tail outcomes1. If an AI connects a geopolitical event to an obscure supply chain without direct external evidence, it trades on an unverified, autoregressive inference.  
To prevent this, every investment thesis must require an explicit, verifiable external evidence trigger before AI reasoning engages1. TBE must operate as a reactive analytical engine rather than a proactive forecasting dreamer. An external trigger—such as a quantified earnings surprise (PEAD), an SEC 8-K filing, a clinical trial result, or a sudden, unexplained volume expansion—must first be flagged by the deterministic code. Only after this hard trigger is detected does the AI spin up to qualitatively read the news, summarize the earnings call transcript, interpret management tone, and explain why the anomaly is occurring58.  
The AI engine genuinely outperforms deterministic code in synthesizing conflicting qualitative narratives59. For example, if a company reports record revenue, deterministic code registers a positive quantitative signal. However, if the CEO's tone during the earnings call indicates severe supply chain anxieties, deterministic code cannot easily weigh these factors. An AI, utilizing a structured prompt framework, can parse the earnings call transcript, extract the underlying executive anxiety, identify the semantic contradiction between the balance sheet and the forward guidance, and output a detailed qualitative risk warning57.

## **Risk Management Architecture: Position Sizing and Portfolio Limits**

A 2–20 day swing trading horizon focused on capital preservation requires institutional-grade risk management. Position sizing, portfolio constraints, and exit strategies must be mathematically isolated from emotional or AI-driven logic, relying entirely on deterministic execution. Studies show that position sizing plays a significantly larger role in trading outcomes than the entry signals themselves, accounting for vast variations in risk-adjusted returns61.  
TBE must utilize Average True Range (ATR) based position sizing. This guarantees that capital allocation dynamically scales inversely to market volatility, equalizing the dollar risk across all assets regardless of their individual price movements17. The deterministic formula calculates position size by dividing the account risk amount by the product of the ATR and a predetermined stop-loss multiplier. For a capital preservation focus, the risk percentage should be rigidly capped at 1.0% to 2.0% of total portfolio equity per trade17. If an asset is highly volatile, resulting in a large ATR, the formula automatically outputs a smaller share quantity. Furthermore, if the broader market regime transitions to high volatility, ATR values naturally expand, algorithmically shrinking all new position sizes and reducing systemic exposure17.  
Standard percentage-based stop-losses fail in swing trading because they ignore the natural volatility of an asset, frequently resulting in premature exits. TBE must implement volatility-based dynamic stops, specifically the Chandelier Exit64. Developed by Chuck Le Beau, the Chandelier Exit hangs a trailing stop-loss from the highest high of the trade (for long positions), utilizing an ATR multiplier63. For a 2–20 day swing horizon, an ATR multiplier of 2.0x to 3.0x is standard, though TBE should lean conservative to cut losses swiftly61. The Chandelier Exit acts as an algorithmic ratchet; as the asset price moves in favor of the trade, the stop-loss moves up to lock in profits, but it never moves down65. If the price reverses and breaches this volatility-defined threshold, the deterministic code executes a market order to sell. The AI is strictly forbidden from intervening, rationalizing, or overriding this exit.  
Diversification fails if a portfolio holds highly correlated assets during a market shock. TBE must constantly calculate the 30-day pairwise rolling correlation of all active positions. If a new candidate asset exhibits a correlation greater than 0.6 with an existing holding, its allocated position size is deterministically reduced by 50%, or the trade is rejected entirely to prevent amplified risk61. Additionally, the system must enforce hard caps limiting sector exposure to no more than 20% of the total portfolio equity to prevent localized industry shocks from creating severe drawdowns67.

## **Drawdown and Capital Preservation Mechanics**

To maintain a profile of \+17% annual return with a maximum drawdown of −3%, TBE must implement portfolio-level techniques that proactively de-risk the portfolio during structural market breakdowns, independent of individual asset stop-losses.  
The regime-detection engine acts as a global circuit breaker. If the Absorption Ratio spikes, indicating fragile, tightly coupled markets, and the Turbulence Index breaches its historic upper percentiles, the system automatically enacts a "Risk-Off" state7. In this state, the system halts all new entries, reflecting an absolute abstention bias. Concurrently, existing Chandelier Exits are tightened, reducing the ATR multiplier to ensure that if the market rolls over, profits are locked in and cash is raised quickly66.  
To mathematically guarantee these risk constraints, TBE should utilize Conformal Risk Control (CRC) and Regime-Weighted Conformal (RWC) calibration for portfolio Value-at-Risk (VaR)4. Traditional VaR relies on normal distribution assumptions that fail catastrophically during fat-tail market events, as evidenced during the 2008 financial crisis69. Conformal prediction provides finite-sample validity guarantees without making any distributional assumptions, constructing prediction intervals that are guaranteed to contain the true value with a specified probability4.  
RWC adapts the safety buffers from past forecast errors using exponential time decay and regime-similarity weights, targeting regime-conditional calibration when market conditions recur4. By tracking the expected loss function via CRC, TBE can adaptively adjust its portfolio-level exposure to guarantee that expected drawdowns remain bounded. This ensures strict control of the tail risk regardless of non-stationary market behavior, directly optimizing for the capital preservation mandate69.

## **Uncertainty Handling and Confidence-Based Abstention**

TBE is uniquely biased toward rejecting opportunities. No-trade periods are fully acceptable and reflect a disciplined approach to risk. To formalize this, the system must handle uncertainty explicitly rather than silently averaging conflicting signals into a mediocre consensus.  
In both quantitative finance and AI-assisted decision systems, the best practice for uncertainty handling is Selective Classification, also known as Conformal Abstention or the "reject option"76. When the deterministic models and the AI qualitative engine evaluate an asset, they do not merely produce a point-prediction. Instead, using conformal prediction, they produce a prediction interval—a range of outcomes guaranteed to contain the true result with a user-specified confidence level72.  
If the resulting prediction interval is too wide, indicating immense uncertainty, or if it spans both significant gains and significant losses, the system explicitly abstains from the trade78. This is operationalized through Selective Conformal Risk Control (SCRC), a two-stage procedure where the system first determines which samples to accept based on a confidence threshold, and then applies conformal risk control to construct calibrated prediction sets only for the accepted samples78.  
Crucially, if the analytical methodologies conflict—for instance, the PEAD indicates a strong fundamental buy, but the technicals are breaking down and the AI flags negative executive sentiment—the system must not average these inputs. Averaging conflicting data masks underlying risk and creates a false sense of security83. Instead, conflicts organically widen the conformal prediction interval84. When the interval exceeds the maximum allowable variance, TBE's SCRC mechanism triggers a deterministic abstention, explicitly logging the data conflict as the reason for rejecting the opportunity86.

## **Architectural "Never" Rules: Lessons from Systemic Failures**

An autonomous system with Level 4 capabilities possesses the speed and authority to destroy a portfolio in minutes if its constraints fail88. By studying the most catastrophic algorithmic and institutional failures in financial history—specifically Long-Term Capital Management (LTCM), Knight Capital, and the JPMorgan London Whale—TBE adopts a series of immutable architectural laws. These rules are hard-coded into the execution environment and cannot be bypassed by any AI agent, market condition, or learning algorithm.  
The collapse of Knight Capital in 2012, which resulted in a $460 million loss in 45 minutes, was caused by a deployment error that triggered dormant "dead code" known as Power Peg. The system lacked output volume monitoring, automated output reconciliation, and an autonomous kill switch to halt aberrant loop behavior89.  
The JPMorgan London Whale incident incurred over $6 billion in losses because traders in the Synthetic Credit Portfolio repeatedly breached Value-at-Risk (VaR) limits. Instead of cutting positions, management implemented a more lenient VaR model to hide the risk. Furthermore, the positions grew so large they exceeded market capacity, becoming completely illiquid when the firm attempted to unwind them93.  
The 1998 failure of LTCM demonstrated the lethal nature of leverage combined with historical correlation assumptions. LTCM utilized massive leverage on trades that were assumed to be uncorrelated based on historical data. However, during the Russian debt default, a systemic flight-to-liquidity caused these supposedly uncorrelated assets to become perfectly correlated, amplifying losses across the entire portfolio88.

| The "Never" Rule | Historical Failure Precedent | TBE Architectural Implementation |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **NEVER deploy live code or logic without a global kill switch and output reconciliation.** | Knight Capital (2012)89 | The execution module must reconcile intended orders against actual market outbounds. If outbound velocity or volume exceeds a predefined baseline, all API access is severed automatically. |
| **NEVER allow a position size to dictate market liquidity (Capacity Limits).** | JPMorgan London Whale (2012)93 | A single position must never exceed a strictly defined fraction (e.g., 1%) of the asset's 30-day Average Daily Volume (ADV). |
| **NEVER override or dynamically loosen a stop-loss or VaR limit once a trade is live.** | JPMorgan London Whale (2012)95 | Stop-losses (Chandelier Exits) can only ratchet in the direction of the trade (locking in profit). The limit can never be moved backwards to give a trade "more room to breathe." |
| **NEVER average down into a losing position.** | Behavioral Finance / Institutional Trading20 | If a trade goes negative, capital exposure cannot be increased under any circumstances. Adding to a loser breaks predetermined sizing math. |
| **NEVER rely on historical correlation holding true during a systemic crisis.** | Long-Term Capital Management (1998)88 | Leverage is strictly prohibited. Portfolio constraints must assume correlations will converge toward 1.0 during regime shifts identified by the Absorption Ratio. |
| **NEVER trade live capital for the purpose of exploration or learning.** | Reinforcement Learning Failures86 | All learning is strictly observational, utilizing accumulated closed-trade data and out-of-sample paper trading to refine parameters for future versions. |

## **Precedents for High-Autonomy Systems**

Level 4 autonomy implies that a system can observe, reason, decide, and act independently across an extended timeframe, adapting to environments without continuous human oversight, relying on humans only for legal, API, or extreme security exceptions98.  
Recent academic and institutional prototypes provide a structural roadmap for TBE. Frameworks like TradingAgents and AlphaCrafter simulate entire trading firms using Multi-Agent Systems (MAS) powered by Large Language Models5. In these systems, distinct agents assume specific roles to enforce a division of labor and prevent confirmation bias. A "Miner" or data agent searches for quantitative inputs; "Analysts" evaluate fundamentals and technicals; "Bull/Bear Researchers" debate the thesis to identify weaknesses; and a "Risk Manager" enforces constraints before executing the trade5.  
However, without strict deterministic boundaries, these high-autonomy systems consistently break down. The primary failure mode is autoregressive drift, or the "agent bullwhip" effect56. As agents converse and pass data, minor probabilistic hallucinations compound, causing upstream variance to severely distort downstream execution1. Systems that allow agents to generate narratives without a deterministic verification floor frequently ingest marketing bias and hallucinate financial math, creating an illusion of competence1.  
To mitigate this, TBE enforces strict structured communication protocols rather than open-ended dialogue. AI outputs must map directly to deterministic data structures (e.g., Pydantic JSON schemas)59. TBE’s absolute separation of AI (qualitative narrative generation) and Code (deterministic math, Z-Score, ATR, M-Score calculation) provides the required structural validation layer, ensuring that the AI can never execute logically impossible trades1.

## **Closing Synthesis: The TBE Decision Framework Shape**

TBE is not a standalone trading strategy; it is a rigid, autonomous framework designed to faithfully automate the discipline of an expert institutional risk manager. By adhering to the constraints provided, the framework operates through a rigorous, linear pipeline that prioritizes capital preservation at every node:

1. **Observe (Deterministic):** The system continuously monitors macroeconomic and cross-asset data. The Regime Detection module (Absorption Ratio, Turbulence Index, VIX) determines the current market environment, automatically adjusting global variables for position sizing, correlation caps, and abstention thresholds7.  
2. **Gather Evidence (Deterministic):** The system scans the market exclusively for 2–20 day swing catalysts, relying primarily on Post-Earnings Announcement Drift (PEAD) and fundamental anomalies to provide an external trigger23.  
3. **Evaluate Risk & Filter (Deterministic):** Triggered candidates are passed through the Tier 1 Hard-Disqualifier list. Any asset exhibiting a delayed 10-K, an auditor resignation, a Beneish M-Score \> \-1.78, an Altman Z-score \< 1.81, or severe illiquidity is instantly and permanently discarded36.  
4. **Reason (AI Interpretive Engine):** For the surviving candidates, the AI engine is activated. It parses earnings transcripts, news filings, and sentiment data, extracting qualitative context and flagging hidden risks that math cannot see. The AI outputs structured, probabilistic scores regarding narrative strength57.  
5. **Score & Conformal Abstention (Deterministic):** The system aggregates the deterministic metrics and AI qualitative scores using equal weights. It applies Conformal Risk Control to generate a prediction interval4. If the interval is too wide, or if the quantitative and qualitative signals conflict, the system abstains76.  
6. **Decide & Size (Deterministic):** For the rare assets that survive all gates and intervals, the deterministic execution engine calculates the position size using the ATR formula, capped by portfolio correlation limits and a strict 1% to 2% risk parameter17.  
7. **Monitor & Exit (Deterministic):** Once live, the trade is governed exclusively by the Chandelier Exit65. The trailing stop ratchets upwards with volatility; if the price reverses, the system executes a market sell. The AI cannot intervene to rationalize holding a loser.  
8. **Learn (Observational):** Closed trades are archived. Observational learning algorithms measure the efficacy of specific AI prompts, regime thresholds, and ATR multipliers, running shadow backtests to propose adjustments for future deployments without ever risking live capital to explore.

This architecture ensures that TBE is fundamentally biased toward inaction. It executes a trade only when a convergence of hard mathematical evidence, clean financial reporting, low systemic fragility, and favorable qualitative narratives aligns perfectly—sacrificing the frequency of returns to absolutely secure the preservation of capital.
